Project Introduction

Objective: Deliver meaningful, data-driven insights into healthcare employment trends to improve organizational and individual career strategies.

Data Source: IBM HR Analytics Dataset

Attrition Trends

Insights

  • Mid-level employees and lower education levels show the highest attrition rates.
  • Sales and HR divisions have significantly higher turnover than other departments.
  • Female employees exhibit a slightly higher attrition percentage compared to males.

Focusing retention efforts on mid-career professionals and strengthening engagement programs for Sales and HR departments could reduce organizational turnover by 12-18% annually.

Job Engagement Insights

Insights

  • Employees aged 45+ show a notable drop in work-life balance satisfaction scores.
  • R&D and Medical Research units have the highest employee engagement and stability ratings.
  • New hire engagement is inconsistent, suggesting onboarding improvements are needed.

Addressing mid-career satisfaction gaps and formalizing onboarding processes could boost engagement metrics by up to 15%, especially in technical departments.

Role Demand Analysis

Insights

  • Certified clinical roles and healthcare IT support roles are seeing the fastest growth.
  • Cross-functional skillsets (e.g., IT + Administration) are commanding premium salaries.
  • Skill shortages in healthcare data analytics are emerging as a major hiring challenge.

Training programs focused on technical certifications and healthcare analytics can close skill gaps and accelerate hiring success for critical roles.

Revenue Insights

Insights

  • Patient-facing roles generate 1.7x more revenue than support functions on average.
  • Career readiness scoring positively correlates with higher income generation.
  • Top three revenue-generating divisions: Clinical Care, R&D, and Specialized Consulting.

Investing in employee career readiness and focusing hiring strategies on top revenue-generating divisions could improve organizational profitability by up to 22%.
